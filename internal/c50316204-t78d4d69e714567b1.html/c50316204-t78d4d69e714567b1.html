The Rules of the Game (Part I)
The expressions in the sequence are evaluated from the right-hand-side to the left-hand-side.

i.e., they're evaluated in this direction : <--------------------

OR, the rightmost expression is evaluated first and the leftmost expression is evaluated last
Chitharanjan! Das - 15 de outubro de 2008
The Rules of the Game (Part V)
So finally, the output should like like:

6 6 6 6 6 6

A short summary of the above mentioned steps:

1. Note down the final value of i, i.e., the one it attains after all the expressions have been evaluated.

2. Start evaluating each expression right to left. Also, keep a note of the current value of i, i.e., the value it holds after the previous expression was evaluated.

3. If the expression is simply i, the final value of i will be printed.

4. If the expression is prefix (++i OR --i), the final value of i will be printed.

5. If the expression is postfix (i++ OR i--), the current value of i will be printed.
Chitharanjan! Das - 15 de outubro de 2008
A Word of CAUTION
Though these rules look too complicated, they have been found to work in many cases. People knowing less complex methods are requested to post them here.

And another thing:

Such evaluation of a sequence of expressions is COMPILER-DEPENDENT, and so, is considered undefined. The Rules of the Game will undoubtedly fail when tried out in Turbo C 3.0.

This is just one of the methods that was found to work with GCC.
