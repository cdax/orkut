small correction ! and final answer ;)
Is it only the property a power of 2 (n), that each bit in the bitpattern of (n-1) will be the NOT of the corresponding bits in the bitpattern of n
.
.
Each bit pattern in (n) is not ('not' here refers to the adverb not in english ) the flip of corresponding bit .. its only the flip of the bits till the least significant '1'
.
eg:
.........n = 001100111000000
.......n-1= 001100110111111

u see, least significant '1' in (n) became '0' and all the bits to its right became one '1', but, rest of the bits are unchanged
.so,
.
.n&(n-1)=001100110000000 (You see.. only the least significant '1' has become '0', rest of the pattern is preserved)
.......
So, the moral of the story is, if we have (n), the pattern (n&(n-1)) is very much similar to (n), except the least significant '1' is cleared . So, if its a power of 2, and clearing least significant bit is nothing but, result is ZERO ( as thr is only '1' in it )..
..
