Chitharanjan! Das - 16 de outubro de 2008
Why nad?
Well, what the first printf() does is pretty clear -- It leaves a blank line and prints abr. The second printf() inserts a backspace character first, i.e., removes the last printed character r and prints sir. Finally, the last printf() clears the entire current line, and takes the cursor to the leftmost position, then prints nad.

So, stepwise after each printf(), the output is as shown:

1
.
abr2
.
absir3
.
nad
Chitharanjan! Das - 16 de outubro de 2008
i.e., the carriage return (\r) not only takes the cursor to the leftmost position, but also clears out the entire line.
